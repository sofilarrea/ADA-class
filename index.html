Legend says...

In the ancient times when sailors roamed the seas in search of distant lands and untold treasures, whispers of ethereal beings began to echo across the waves. Tales spoke of encounters with captivating creatures—half-human, half-fish—known as mermaids. Legend has it that these mesmerizing beings dwelled in the deepest, most hidden corners of the ocean, their songs captivating sailors and guiding lost ships to safety. Sailors recounted enchanting melodies that would drift over the waves, a symphony that could soothe the fiercest of storms and calm the roughest of seas. Stories spread among seafaring communities—of sailors returning from their voyages, wide-eyed and eager to share their encounters with these otherworldly beings. They described the gleaming tails of vibrant colors, cascading hair adorned with pearls and shells, and eyes that sparkled like the ocean itself.

Oceana

Nerissa

Meaning "from the sea," a name that resonates with mermaid lore and their oceanic habitat

Pearlina
